## Summary
Argues that a **shared breakdown structure** between disciplines and documents increases
traceability and predictability, bridging idealized work-preparation plans and actual site
execution. Two rooftop-assembly case studies (Case 1 with a second data-collection phase of
interviews + recorded roof-assembly observation; comparative Case 3). Interoperability
between cost, schedule and documentation emerges only when the shared breakdown is actually
*used* to detail assemblies.

## Key takeaways
- The breakdown structure is the load-bearing artifact: detail assemblies against a shared
  structure or the plan↔execution link breaks.
- Work-preparation planning (Swedish *arbetsberedning*) is where the takt/zone structure
  must reach down to executable detail.

## Distinct contribution
The execution/work-preparation slice of the TBS programme — shows the breakdown structure's
value at the detailing-and-handover level, not just at planning level.
