# haghsheno-2016-history

- **Title:** History and Theoretical Foundations of Takt Planning and Takt Control
- **Authors / Year:** Haghsheno, S.; Binninger, M.; Dlouhy, J.; Sterlike, S. — 2016
- **Venue:** IGLC 24, Boston, USA · [landing](https://iglc.net/papers/Details/1297) · OA [PDF](https://iglc.net/Papers/Details/1297/pdf)
- **Status:** adopted · **Cluster:** takt-foundations · **Verified:** ✔

## Summary
Literature review establishing the theoretical foundations of takt: traces "takt"
from stationary (manufacturing) production into construction, connects it to core
Lean goals (prevent overproduction, reduce lead time, short-cycle control), and
surveys the German practice lineage. Notes Bulhões et al. (2005) as the first IGLC use.

## Key takeaways
- Takt = a fixed unit of time defining production rhythm; in construction the "station"
  is a spatial zone rather than a moving line.
- Distinguishes takt **planning** (design the rhythm) from takt **control** (hold it).
- Genealogy root (Bulhões 2005) is **cited but unverified** here — see INDEX gap #4.

## Distinct contribution
The best definitions-and-origins anchor for the takt vocabulary.

## Overlap / what it is NOT
Theory/history, not method — distinct from the same authors' `dlouhy-2016-three-level`
(zone hierarchy) and `binninger-2017-technical-takt` (control mechanics).

## How it shapes taktology (intertwine)
Source of the definitions in [`docs/02-vocabulary.md`](../../docs/02-vocabulary.md)
(takt, zone-as-station, planning vs control). Flags that the wagon/train metaphor and
genealogy root are **not** formally sourced (INDEX gaps #3, #4) — so those parts of the
vocabulary doc are labelled our synthesis, not cited fact.
