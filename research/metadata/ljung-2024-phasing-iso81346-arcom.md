## Summary
Argues that standardized information exchange between disciplines is hampered by missing
interconnection capabilities, and proposes using **ISO/IEC 81346** reference-designation /
classification to add **phasing information** that links disciplines. Connects coding/
classification standards to schedule phasing — the coding layer beneath the TBS.

## Key takeaways
- A standardized **reference-designation scheme** (ISO 81346) can carry phasing and link
  product/process/location aspects across disciplines.
- This is the "multi-aspect coding and referencing logic" component of the TBS made concrete.

## Distinct contribution
The standards/coding angle of the TBS programme — how to *identify* and cross-reference
spatio-temporal units interoperably.

## Overlap / what it is NOT

## How it shapes taktology (intertwine)
**Directly relevant to taktology's open identifier/namespace item.** taktology currently
relies on `w3id.org/taktology` IRIs and ad-hoc zone ids (e.g. `B5:1`); ISO 81346 offers a
principled, standards-based scheme for stable **zone/phase identifiers** — a candidate for
how `takt:TaktZone` / `takt:TaktTask` instances should be named for cross-discipline
interoperability. Flagged in [INDEX.md](../INDEX.md) Cluster F overlap note.
