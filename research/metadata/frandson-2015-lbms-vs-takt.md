# frandson-2015-lbms-vs-takt

- **Title:** Comparison Between Location Based Management and Takt Time Planning
- **Authors / Year:** Frandson, A. G.; Seppänen, O.; Tommelein, I. D. — 2015
- **Venue:** IGLC 23, Perth, Australia · [landing](https://iglc.net/Papers/Details/1181) · OA [PDF](https://iglc.net/Papers/Details/1181/pdf)
- **Status:** adopted · **Cluster:** location-based · **Verified:** ✔

## Summary
The head-to-head comparison of two space-as-resource paradigms. Both LBMS and takt
seek balanced, conflict-free schedules, but LBMS prioritizes **continuous crew flow
with time buffers and forecasting**, whereas takt fixes a **continuous process per
location using capacity buffers and off-takt work**.

## Key takeaways
- LBMS optimizes crew flow across locations; takt fixes a rhythm per zone — they look
  alike but buffer and pace differently.
- The two are routinely conflated; this paper exists to keep them apart.

## Distinct contribution
The single best source for disentangling LBMS from takt — the scoping reference.

## Overlap / what it is NOT
NOT an endorsement of either; a comparison. Pair with `kenley-seppanen-2010-lbms-book`
(LBMS canon) when the boundary matters.

## How it shapes taktology (intertwine)
Scopes what taktology models. Justifies keeping `takt:` strictly a **takt** vocabulary
(fixed rhythm per zone, capacity buffers) rather than importing LBMS flowline concepts
— recorded in the Cluster-B overlap-risk note in [INDEX.md](../INDEX.md) and the scope
framing of [`docs/02-vocabulary.md`](../../docs/02-vocabulary.md).
