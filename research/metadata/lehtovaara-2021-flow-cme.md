# lehtovaara-2021-flow-cme

- **Title:** How Takt Production Contributes to Construction Production Flow: A Theoretical Model
- **Authors / Year:** Lehtovaara, J.; Seppänen, O.; Peltokorpi, A.; Kujansuu, P.; Grönvall, M. — 2021
- **Venue:** Construction Management and Economics, 39(1), 73–95 · DOI [10.1080/01446193.2020.1824295](https://doi.org/10.1080/01446193.2020.1824295) · OA [Aaltodoc](https://aaltodoc.aalto.fi/handle/123456789/102817)
- **Status:** adopted · **Cluster:** implementation-cases · **Verified:** ✔

## Summary
Builds a theoretical model linking takt production to construction production flow via
qualitative multi-case study. Finds takt is viable for increasing flow but demands
heavy planning/control/continuous-improvement effort, with positive **location** flow
but contradictory **operations (trade)** flow effects.

## Key takeaways
- Two flows to distinguish: **location flow** (work moving smoothly through a zone) vs
  **trade flow** (a crew moving smoothly between zones). Takt favours the former and
  can strain the latter.
- Benefits are real but not free — control effort is the cost.

## Distinct contribution
The conceptual frame separating location flow from trade flow — names *what* a takt
model is optimizing and the tension it creates.

## Overlap / what it is NOT
A theory synthesis, not a single case study — don't cite as one. Synthesizes the IGLC
cases it references.

## How it shapes taktology (intertwine)
Frames the "two halves, one graph" rationale in
[`docs/01-architecture.md`](../../docs/01-architecture.md): location flow ≈ the
`hasSuccessor` train through a zone; trade flow ≈ a wagon's run across zones (Reading B).
Explains *why* both readings of "train" matter and are worth representing.
